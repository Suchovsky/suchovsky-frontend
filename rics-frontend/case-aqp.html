As the lead designer and developer for project AQP, it was my responsibility to completely redesign and restructure the client's previous website. The objective was making information on the site more palatable for potential customers.

In the initial stage, I collaborated with my colleague from Content department and we drafted rough structure of the site. I then used this draft to create high fidelity wireframe which was then presented to the client. Upon review with the client I adopted the necessary changes and developed the wireframe into first version of the design that consisted of the core pages of the site.

After finalization of the complete design, I started the development stage that I was fully responsible for. This stage was divided into two main objectives: frontend and backend. At the point of finalized frontend development, I then transferred source code into our CMS of choice [ProcessWire]. This solution was offered to a client for its ease of managing content on their new website as well as safety and fast load speeds.

Last thing that remained at this point was showcasing final product to the client and familiarising them with the CMS and producing handy manual for navigating admin interface.

Update: The client's satisfaction led us to develop three more websites for their subsidiary companies using this custom solution.
